Travel to Skopje

The main airport is Skopje International Airport. Turkish Airlines offers convenient connections via Istanbul (especially from the West Coast), and many major European hubs also connect to Skopje. You may also consider Ohrid St. Paul The Apostle Airport (about 2.5 hours from Skopje), particularly if you plan to spend time in Ohrid.

If you’re extending your stay to explore Macedonia (highly recommended!), renting a car at the airport is a great option. Parking in Skopje is manageable, and a car makes it easy to take day trips or drive to Ohrid.

Most hotels offer airport shuttle service to the city center (approximately 30–40 minutes drive).

Alternatively, an airport shuttle bus runs to the city center (around $4 per ticket). You can find the timetable here.

Taxis to the city center typically cost $25–30. If you send us your arrival details, we’re happy to help arrange a taxi in advance.

Things to Do

There is plenty to do in Skopje. Here are some suggestions for sightseeing:

  • Skopje Old Town: Old Turkish bazaar with small shops, old mosques and churches, good shopping.
  • Skopje Fortress (Kale): Byzantine Fortress from 11th century
  • Hike to the Millennium Cross: Cable car ride up mount Vodno, view of the city, and big cross

Even more to do if willing to travel outside of Skopje (highly recommend!). Here are some suggestions for longer trips:

  • Canyon Matka and Matka Caves: One of the world's deepest underwater caves!
  • Lazar Winery
  • Aurora Resort and Spa - Berovo
  • Ancient Town and Archaeological Site of Stobi: Ancient site and a nice Stobi winery nearby
  • St. John the Baptist Monastery: Monastery and church dating back to the year 1020
  • Macedonian Ethno Village: Restaurant, hotel, shopping strip in traditional Macedonian style

Places to Visit in Macedonia

If you have extra time, consider visiting Ohrid (one of the world's deepest and oldest natural lakes). We highly recommend extending your trip to visit this lake city. Here is more on Ohrid and must-sees:

  • Kaneo: Historic, landmark Orthodox church overlooking Lake Ohrid
  • St. Naum Monastery and Ostrovo Restaurant: Eastern Orthodox monastery founded in 905
  • Church of Hagia Sophia: Middle Ages Church
  • The Bay of Bones: Reconstructed village of stilt houses displaying Bronze & Iron Age items recovered from Lake Ohrid
  • Party Beach Bar Gradishte: Three connected beaches with bars and day parties
  • Restaurant Kajche: Restaurant by the lake's boardwalk

General Tips and Other Info

  • Children are very welcome at the wedding in Skopje. Like that kid at Bad Bunny’s Super Bowl wedding, kids tend to have a blast running around, dancing, and eventually falling asleep on chairs under the tables :)
  • Macedonians are warm, hospitable, and friendly. However, in public spaces they may not appear overly expressive. Don’t expect many verbal pleasantries like “please,” “excuse me,” or “thank you,” or lots of smiling at strangers.
  • The official currency is the Macedonian denar, though euros are often accepted. Paying by card is usually best to avoid unfavorable exchange rates. Apple Pay and card terminals are widely available.
  • Tipping is appreciated but modest. It’s not 20% — a small amount (e.g., €1 after coffee/drinks or up to €5 for a larger meal) is more than sufficient. Tips are typically left on the table after paying.
  • Be mindful of pickpockets (often kids or teenagers), especially around the main square, City Park, and the Old Bazaar.
  • Smoking is common. While many indoor venues (including our restaurant) prohibit it, you may still encounter indoor smoking in some places. Apologies in advance for any secondhand smoke; it’s part of the cultural experience, unfortunately.
